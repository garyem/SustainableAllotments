Introduction to Deep Bed Farming

Deep Bed Farming (DBF) is a simple and natural way of gardening. It gives plants more room to grow by making the soil deeper, healthier, and easier to manage. It's especially useful on UK allotments.

‚úÖ Why Deep Bed Farming is a Smart Move for a UK Allotment

1. Improved Drainage in a Rain-Prone Climate

Raised deep beds help prevent waterlogging by letting rain drain down through the soil. At the same time, they hold onto enough moisture for plants to thrive.

2. Combatting Soil Compaction

Deep beds break through compacted soil (called hardpan), letting roots, water, and air reach deeper. This means healthier and stronger plants.

3. Maximising Limited Space

With better soil and root depth, you can grow more in a small area. That means more food from less space.

4. Boosting Soil Fertility Organically

Deep beds let you add compost and rich materials easily, helping you grow healthy, strong crops without chemicals.

5. Extended Growing Season

Raised beds warm up faster in spring, so you can plant earlier and grow for longer ‚Äî a big bonus in the UK!

6. Easier to Manage (and Kinder on the Back!)

Raised beds are easier to reach and care for. You can weed, water, and harvest without bending too far.
